Born and bred in Beverly Hills, California, young Edward Tufte was interested in politics and statistics. It was not unexpected, as his father worked in the city council, while his mother was a distinguished emeritum English professor.

In 1968,Tufte began teaching at Princeton University after earning a degree in political science  at Stanford University.

During the next two decades, Tufte established milestones in both graphic design and data science.

The college course was turned into large joint seminars and, eventually, into one-day classes drawing phenomenal interest among the staff and students.

His four books have made Tufte not only the world’s leading statistic analyst but also a celebrated communication designer.

On March 5, 2010, President Barack Obama appointed Tufte to the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act's Recovery Independent Advisory Panel "to provide transparency in the use of Recovery-related funds".

Approaching his seventies, Tufte is currently writing his fhfth book,consulting for NASA, and building sculptures at his 234ac farm.
